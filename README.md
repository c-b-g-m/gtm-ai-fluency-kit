# GTM AI Fluency Kit

Most GTM professionals can't describe what "good" AI use looks like for their role. They're using it reactively — one-off prompts, first-pass outputs, no quality criteria. They know it isn't enough. They just don't have a clear picture of what to do differently.

This kit gives you that picture, and a path to get there.

**After completing this kit, you will know:**
1. Where you actually stand — scored against observable behaviors, not vibes
2. What to work on first — your two lowest dimensions become your 30-day focus
3. How to lead your team through the same process — in 30 minutes, with a structure that works

It's free, role-specific, and takes about 30–45 minutes for a first pass. Run it once a quarter to track movement — it's a baseline, not a finish line.

**What makes this different from generic AI advice:** Most frameworks ask whether you use AI or how often. This one asks what you can demonstrate right now — a prompt library, a decision log, a checklist you actually use. If you can't point to a real artifact, you don't claim the score. That's the standard that actually moves people forward.

## What This Is Not

- Not a tool evaluation. What tools you use doesn't affect your score.
- Not a test of enthusiasm or effort. Someone who uses AI rarely but intentionally scores higher than someone who uses it constantly without criteria.
- Not enterprise-scale automation builds or multi-team dependency management. The 60/90-day plans reference building lightweight, self-owned workflows — things within your individual scope to try, own, and measure.
- Not a verbatim reproduction of any vendor's curriculum — a practical synthesis built for GTM roles specifically, based on real patterns observed working with GTM teams.

## The 4D Framework

The assessment measures fluency across four dimensions — all observable behaviors, not attitudes or intentions.

| Dimension | What It Measures |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Delegation** | Knowing what to give to AI, what to keep human, and why |
| **Description** | How well you communicate with AI to get useful output |
| **Discernment** | Catching what AI gets wrong before it causes a problem |
| **Diligence** | Staying accountable for what AI produces on your behalf |

Each dimension is scored 1–3. The scoring standard: **don't claim a 3 unless you can point to a concrete artifact** — a prompt library, a decision log, a checklist in active use. A 3 means you can demonstrate it, not just describe it.

## Who Built This

I'm [Christen George-McFerrin](https://demandai.studio), founder of demand AI studio. I built this framework for myself first.

I was trying to answer a question I couldn't find a good answer to anywhere: *was I spending my learning time productively, in ways I could bring back to my role?* I needed something that would tell me whether I was working for change or working for activity. A learning plan moored to where I actually was — not where I aspired to be — and specific to my functional role turned out to be the thing that made AI skill-building feel real rather than performative.

I started sharing it with friends who were AI-curious but didn't know where or how to start. In that beta period, I've helped people move from treating AI like a better search bar to building repeatable workflows and, in some cases, full automations. The pattern that kept showing up: people didn't need more tools or tutorials — they needed a clear picture of where they were and a concrete plan for what to change first.

This kit is that picture, made open and reusable. It's early — I'm actively incorporating feedback. If you use it, I'd genuinely like to know what happened.
