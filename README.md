# GTM AI Fluency Kit

Most GTM professionals can't describe what "good" AI use looks like for their role. They're using it reactively — one-off prompts, first-pass outputs, no quality criteria. They know it isn't enough. They just don't have a clear picture of what to do differently.

This kit gives you that picture, and a path to get there.

**After completing this kit, you will know:**
1. Where you actually stand — scored against observable behaviors, not vibes
2. What to work on first — your two lowest dimensions become your 30-day focus
3. How to lead your team through the same process — in 30 minutes, with a structure that works

It's free, role-specific, and takes 20–30 minutes for a first pass.

---

## Who This Is For

**Individual contributors** who want to know specifically what "better" looks like for their role — not generic AI tips.

**Team leads** who want a shared baseline and common language before running an internal AI fluency session.

**Change leaders** who need to assess their own fluency first, then bring the framework to their team. Start with `QUICKSTART-MANUAL.md`, then read `FACILITATOR-LITE.md`.

Function tracks: Marketing · Sales and RevOps · Support and Customer Success

## What This Is Not

- Not a tool evaluation. What tools you use doesn't affect your score.
- Not a test of enthusiasm or effort. Someone who uses AI rarely but intentionally scores higher than someone who uses it constantly without criteria.
- Not consulting playbooks, client-specific materials, or enterprise automation tooling.
- Not a verbatim reproduction of any vendor's curriculum — a practical synthesis built for GTM roles specifically.

## The 4D Framework

The assessment measures fluency across four dimensions — all observable behaviors, not attitudes or intentions.

| Dimension | What It Measures |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Delegation** | Knowing what to give to AI, what to keep human, and why |
| **Description** | How well you communicate with AI to get useful output |
| **Discernment** | Catching what AI gets wrong before it causes a problem |
| **Diligence** | Staying accountable for what AI produces on your behalf |

Each dimension is scored 1–3. The scoring standard: **don't claim a 3 unless you can point to a concrete artifact** — a prompt library, a decision log, a checklist in active use. A 3 means you can demonstrate it, not just describe it.
