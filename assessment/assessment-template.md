## Section A: Delegation

1. Which recurring tasks do you currently offload to AI?
2. Which tasks remain fully human-owned, and why?
3. What criteria do you use to decide when AI should or should not be involved?

Evidence prompt:
- Provide one workflow map (simple list is fine) for a recurring task.

## Section B: Description

1. Show a representative prompt pattern you use repeatedly.
2. What context do you provide before requesting output?
3. How do you adjust when initial output misses the mark?

Evidence prompt:
- Provide one before/after prompt sequence and explain what changed.

## Section C: Discernment

1. How do you define quality for outputs in your function?
2. What checks do you run before using or sharing AI output?
3. Share one example where you caught and corrected an AI issue.

Evidence prompt:
- Provide a keep/edit/discard decision log for one output.

## Section D: Diligence

1. How do you disclose AI involvement when relevant?
2. What privacy/risk checks do you run by default?
3. Who is accountable for final outputs and decisions?

Evidence prompt:
- Provide a short diligence statement for one recent AI-assisted task.

## Section E: Progression Slope

1. What was your AI workflow like 6 months ago?
2. What is different now?
3. What did you stop doing?
4. What systems or habits have become repeatable?

Evidence prompt:
- Provide one timeline with at least 3 milestones.

## Calibration Anchors (Use Before Final Scoring)

- **1 (Emerging)**: mostly one-off usage, weak evidence, limited quality controls.
- **2 (Developing)**: some repeatability, partial criteria, inconsistent diligence.
- **3 (Capable)**: repeatable workflows, explicit quality criteria, accountable review habits.
- **4 (Adoptive)**: durable systems, shared patterns, embedded governance checks.

If evidence is weak or missing, lower score by one level.
