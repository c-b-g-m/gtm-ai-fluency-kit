## Section A: Delegation

1. Which recurring tasks do you currently offload to AI?
2. Which tasks remain fully human-owned, and why?
3. What criteria do you use to decide when AI should or should not be involved?

Evidence prompt:
- Provide one workflow map (simple list is fine) for a recurring task.

## Section B: Description

1. Show a representative prompt pattern you use repeatedly.
2. What context do you provide before requesting output?
3. How do you adjust when initial output misses the mark?

Evidence prompt:
- Provide one before/after prompt sequence and explain what changed.

## Section C: Discernment

1. How do you define quality for outputs in your function?
2. What checks do you run before using or sharing AI output?
3. Share one example where you caught and corrected an AI issue.

Evidence prompt:
- Provide a keep/edit/discard decision log for one output.

## Section D: Diligence

1. How do you disclose AI involvement when relevant?
2. What privacy/risk checks do you run by default?
3. Who is accountable for final outputs and decisions?

Evidence prompt:
- Provide a short diligence statement for one recent AI-assisted task.

**What is a diligence statement?** 3–5 sentences that describe: what AI was used for, what data went into the prompt, who reviewed the output before it was used or shared, and what you'd do differently next time. Before writing yours, read `examples/diligence-statement-examples.md` — it has one example per function track plus a "weak vs. strong" contrast so you can see the difference.

## Scoring Summary

Score each dimension 1–3 using the scale below. Fill in your score, then note the one artifact that supports it.

| Score | What it means | Evidence requirement |
|-------|--------------|----------------------|
| **1 — Emerging** | Reactive, one-off use. No repeatable process. Limited quality controls. | No artifact required, but be honest: if you're here, say so. |
| **2 — Developing** | Some patterns forming. Inconsistent criteria. Diligence applied sometimes but not by default. | Can point to at least one example, even if not systematic. |
| **3 — Capable** | Repeatable workflows. Explicit quality criteria. Accountable habits applied consistently. | Must point to a real artifact you can open right now — a saved prompt, a decision log, a checklist in active use. |
