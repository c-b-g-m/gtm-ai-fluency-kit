# Worked Example — Marketing

This example shows a completed assessment and learning plan for a marketing individual contributor. Use it as a reference when filling in your own templates.

---

## Participant Context

- **Name:** Alex
- **Role:** Marketing Manager
- **Function:** Marketing
- **JD summary:** Owns demand generation programs including campaign briefing, email sequence development, and cross-channel launch coordination.
- **Role scope:** Full ownership of campaign brief through launch QA. Partners with design and analytics; does not own paid media budget.
- **Role success metrics:** MQL volume, email open and click rates, campaign launch cycle time.
- **Primary recurring workflow:** Weekly email campaign brief — from audience brief to approved copy.
- **Target outcome:** Reduce email campaign brief cycle time from 5 days to 3 days within 60 days.

---

## Baseline Scores

| Dimension | Score | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Delegation | 2 | Uses AI for subject line brainstorming and first-draft copy, but no documented criteria for what goes to AI vs. stays human. Varies by deadline pressure. |
| Description | 2 | Prompts include audience type and product name. Revises when tone is off but has no saved templates. Each prompt is written from scratch. |
| Discernment | 2 | Reads output before using. Catches obvious tone issues. No formal checklist; quality criteria are implicit. |
| Diligence | 2 | Knows not to include customer PII in prompts. Has not documented this as a policy. No consistent disclosure practice for AI-assisted copy. |

**Evidence available:** Two before/after subject line samples. No prompt library. No decision logs.

---

## Priority Gaps

1. **Description (2 → 3):** No reusable prompt structure. Every campaign starts from scratch, which is the main driver of long cycle time.
2. **Diligence (2 → 3):** No disclosure or data-handling practice documented. Risk increases as AI use grows.

---

## Stop Doing / Start Doing

Stop doing:
- Writing campaign prompts from scratch each week with no template.
- Using AI output without a defined review pass.

Start doing:
- Maintaining a prompt library with 3–5 reusable patterns for email campaigns.
- Running a defined review checklist (brand voice, factual accuracy, audience fit) before approving copy.

---

## 30/60/90 Plan

### 30 Days — Foundation

- Build a prompt library with at least 3 patterns: subject line, email body, campaign brief intro.
- Define a 5-point review checklist for email copy (brand voice, factual accuracy, audience fit, CTA clarity, compliance flag).
- Submit one decision log per week documenting what was kept, edited, or discarded and why.

**Evidence:** Prompt library v1 (saved file), 4 decision logs, before/after samples from 2 campaigns.
**JD alignment:** Directly improves campaign brief quality and launch QA, both core to role scope.

### 60 Days — Operationalization

- Standardize the prompt library into the weekly campaign brief workflow — every brief starts with the template.
- Measure cycle time before and after. Target: 5 days → 3 days.
- Run the review checklist on every AI-assisted piece before handoff to design.

**Evidence:** Before/after cycle time snapshot, review checklist in active use, updated prompt library v2.
**JD alignment:** Reduces campaign launch cycle time (listed success metric).

### 90 Days — Scale

- Share the prompt library and review checklist with one other team member.
- Document a one-page AI disclosure standard for campaign copy (what triggers a disclosure, where it goes).
- Present before/after results in a team retrospective.

**Evidence:** Shared prompt library, disclosure standard doc, team retro notes.
**JD alignment:** Peer enablement extends quality improvement beyond individual output.

---

## Non-Generic Check

- Workflow named: weekly email campaign brief ✓
- Measurable signal: campaign brief cycle time (5 days → 3 days in 60 days) ✓
- Accountability owner: Alex owns review and approval before handoff to design ✓
- JD alignment: maps to campaign launch cycle time and launch QA scope ✓
