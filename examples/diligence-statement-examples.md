# Diligence Statement Examples

A diligence statement is a short artifact — 3–5 sentences — attached to a deliverable or included in a handoff note. It covers three things: what AI did, what data stayed out of prompts, and who owns the output.

Write it in first person. Be specific. The point is accountability, not compliance.

---

## What to Cover

1. **What AI did** — the specific task (draft, summarize, research, etc.)
2. **What you provided** — context given to AI; what shaped the output
3. **What stayed out of prompts** — sensitive data, customer info, proprietary data that was excluded
4. **What you reviewed and changed** — the actual check you ran and what you caught
5. **Ownership** — a clear, declarative statement that you own the output

---

## Marketing — Campaign Email Copy

> AI wrote the first draft of all three subject lines and email bodies. I provided the audience brief, campaign goal, tone constraints, and one competitor example to avoid. Before handoff I ran the full content review checklist — revised subject line 2 (off-brand tone), removed one unsupported product claim, no compliance flags. No customer data, list segments, or performance metrics were in any prompt. I reviewed and approved this copy and own every claim in it.

---

## Sales — Pre-Call Research

> This account summary was AI-generated from public sources only: LinkedIn, the company website, and two press releases. No CRM records, deal history, or previous call notes were in any prompt. I verified the three factual claims in the "recent signals" section before the call — corrected one. I own the accuracy of everything I said in that meeting.

---

## Support — Ticket Response

> AI drafted this response. Prompt input was the ticket category, issue type, and the relevant policy excerpt — nothing else. Customer name, account tier, and case history were not included. I ran the standard review: accuracy against the policy doc, tone, escalation trigger check. Rewrote the third paragraph — original was too apologetic for a policy explanation. I sent this. I own it.

---

## What a Weak Diligence Statement Looks Like

For contrast — this is what level 1 looks like:

> *"Used AI to help write this. Reviewed before sending."*

No specifics on what data was or wasn't included. No named checks. No accountability. This is a checkbox, not a statement.
