# Worked Example — Sales and RevOps

This example shows a completed assessment and learning plan for a Sales and RevOps professional. Use it as a reference when filling in your own templates.

---

## Participant Context

- **Name:** Jordan
- **Role:** Account Executive
- **Function:** Sales and RevOps
- **JD summary:** Manages a book of mid-market accounts through full sales cycle — discovery through close — with a focus on pipeline generation and deal velocity.
- **Role scope:** Full ownership of outbound prospecting, discovery calls, and proposal delivery. Partners with solutions engineering on technical discovery. Does not own contract terms.
- **Role success metrics:** Quota attainment, pipeline coverage ratio, average deal cycle length.
- **Primary recurring workflow:** Pre-call research and discovery prep for new prospects.
- **Target outcome:** Reduce pre-call prep time by 40% without reducing quality of discovery questions or account context.

---

## Baseline Scores

| Dimension | Score | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Delegation | 2 | Uses AI for account summaries before calls but the process is informal — skips it when busy. No documented criteria for when to use it. |
| Description | 3 | Has a working prompt structure for account research: company context + recent news + ICP fit + suggested questions. Uses it consistently and revises based on output quality. |
| Discernment | 2 | Checks AI-generated account facts against LinkedIn and the company website. No checklist; relies on memory of what to verify. Has caught errors but doesn't log them. |
| Diligence | 2 | Does not include customer data in prompts. Has not documented this or shared it as a practice. No disclosure habit for AI-assisted account notes. |

**Evidence available:** Three saved prompt templates in notes app. No verification log. No formal disclosure practice.

---

## Priority Gaps

1. **Delegation (2 → 3):** Workflow exists informally but isn't repeatable — skipped when pressed for time. Need documented criteria and a committed practice.
2. **Discernment (2 → 3):** Fact-checking is happening but uncaptured. Building a correction log would surface patterns in AI errors and improve future prompts.

---

## Stop Doing / Start Doing

Stop doing:
- Running prep ad hoc — sometimes with AI, sometimes without, depending on bandwidth.
- Catching AI errors without logging them.

Start doing:
- Running the same pre-call workflow on every qualified opportunity, regardless of time pressure.
- Keeping a weekly correction log of AI errors caught during prep.

---

## 30/60/90 Plan

### 30 Days — Foundation

- Document the current pre-call prep workflow as a repeatable process (not just a saved prompt — a checklist with steps).
- Log every AI error caught during prep for 4 weeks: what was wrong, what the correct information was.
- Define a 3-step verification pass: company facts → contact role/history → recent news or trigger event.

**Evidence:** Written workflow checklist, 4-week correction log, updated prompt templates.
**JD alignment:** Pre-call quality directly supports discovery call effectiveness and pipeline generation.

### 60 Days — Operationalization

- Run the documented workflow on every qualified opportunity for 30 consecutive days.
- Compare pre-call prep time before and after: target 40% reduction.
- Review correction log for patterns — update prompt templates to address the most common error type.

**Evidence:** Prep time before/after log, updated prompt templates v2, correction log analysis.
**JD alignment:** Prep time reduction maps to deal cycle length (listed success metric).

### 90 Days — Scale

- Share the pre-call workflow with two other AEs on the team.
- Build a short guide: "How I use AI for account prep" — what's in the prompt, what you verify, what you never include.
- Present correction log patterns at a team meeting as input for shared prompt improvements.

**Evidence:** Shared workflow guide, team presentation notes, peer adoption confirmation.
**JD alignment:** Team adoption improves pipeline coverage quality across the pod.

## Non-Generic Check

- Workflow named: pre-call research and discovery prep ✓
- Measurable signal: pre-call prep time (40% reduction in 60 days) ✓
- Accountability owner: Jordan owns all prep and verification before each call ✓
- JD alignment: maps to deal cycle length and pipeline generation scope ✓
